Movies

I very rarely watch movies, but here are some of my favorites.

The Apu TrilogyA Bildungsroman about Apu, a high caste but poor young Bengali.
AkiraA post-apocalyptic cyberpunk anime.
Castle in the SkyA young boy and a girl search for a legendary floating castle.
Children Who Chase Lost VoicesA story about love bringing people to Agartha.
SonatineBetrayal, friendship and violence. Takeshi Kitano.
Hana-bi (Fireworks)Violence and love. Takeshi Kitano.
Farewell My ConcubineA friendship spanning nearly 70 years during China's troubled 20th century.
To LiveA portrayal of Chinese people living under social pressures.

Some more ... Japanese movies: everything by Takeshi Kitano, Studio Ghibli and Makoto Shinkai. Chinese language movies: Zhang Yimou (Raise the Red Lantern, To Live, Shanghai Triad, Hero) and Chen Kaige (Farewell My Concubine, Temptress Moon). English language movies: the Lord of the Rings trilogy, 2001: A Space Odyssey and The Lighthouse.

Most H*llywood movies are disappointing although there are some great ones like The Departed and Zodiac, Blade Runner, Drive, plus I will make one exception for shitty comedy when it comes to Walk Hard: The Dewey Cox Story.

Books

  • Fyodor Dostoevsky - The Brothers Karamazov
  • Leo Tolstoy - War and Peace
  • Haruki Murakami - A Wild Cheep Chase
  • Haruki Murakami - Norwegian Wood
  • Vladimir Nabokov - Lolita
  • Friedrich Nietzsche - (all his books)
    It's not possible to understand his philosophy by selecting single aphorisms or even single books. My favorites are The Gay Science and Twilight of the Idols. As for his major works, here is my suggested reading order: The Birth of Tragedy (parts 1-10), Twilight of the Idols, Beyond Good and Evil, On the Genealogy of Morality, The Antichrist, The Gay Science, Ecce Homo, and finally Thus Spoke Zarathustra. If you're reading in English, get the Walter Kaufmann translations.
