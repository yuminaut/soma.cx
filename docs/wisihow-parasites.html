All about worm Infestations

As repulsive as it is, there are no escaping intestinal parasites. These minute organisms have learned to adapt and evolve with man even as his environment, lifestyle, and diet have changed. Incredible as it seems, these parasites manage to make their way into the human digestive system so they can continue their own life cycle, and propagate their species. Intestinal worms are more common in the tropics and subtropical areas. They usually become more prevalent during the rainy season. More often it's children who fall victim to intestinal worms because they tend to put anything in their mouths without washing their hands first. These unhygienic practices, lead to parasites that are single-celled to complex multi-cellular organisms such as intestinal worms. It is said that about 75 percent of the world's population is infected with worms and other parasites. When untreated, these infestations can result in serious illnesses and even death, especially in developing countries.

The eggs of intestinal parasites usually enter the human body by means of water and contaminated food. Undercooked meat is also a major cause of parasitic infestations. Microscopic parasites are not only a result of poor hygienic practices, but can be caused by consuming raw fruits or vegetables that have not been washed properly, and by handling contaminated soil. Parasites are very fond of staying in your body if you eat unhealthy and sugary foods.

It's very common for people to be oblivious and completely unaware they are infested. It will help curb the infestation of intestinal worms if you have your beloved pets de-wormed regularly. Parasitic worms should be prevented from invading your household and your body. To know if you have intestinal worms, it is best to see your doctor for proper tests and diagnose. There are de-worming medications available, but many people prefer all-natural, organic solutions for their worm infestations. One of these potent natural remedies is garlic.

Your digestive tract's colonic system may be lacking in good bacteria, allowing the bad bacteria to take over your digestive process. If you suffer from a pathogenic takeover, your gastrointestinal tract will no longer be able to break down and assimilate the necessary nutrients that your entire body needs to function optimally.

Issues w digestion can result in inflammation and pain. Viruses and bacteria may have entered your body and established themselves in your digestive tract. If after you've noticed the symptoms, it may become worse and worse to a point where it debilitates you. Your doctor may prescribe medications for you that will target the pain, inflammation, and infections. However, side effects are always an issue when you take these artificially produced medications. That is why natural remedies such as garlic are much more preferred.

What garlic contains

Garlic's potency depends on its actual form when you consume it. If you have a garlic clove that is just peeled but still intact and raw, the alliin (amino acid derivative) in it is still there. The moment you chop, crush, or mince the garlic, alliin joins alliinase to form allicin, which gives the herb its healing properties, flavor, and aroma. When you ingest garlic, it enters your stomach, and its compounds begin to dissolve. It then moves to the small intestine where it will be broken down further. Absorption takes place in the small intestine. The cells of your body assimilate the compounds of garlic through your bloodstream. Then, the healing begins. Fresh garlic may be pungent, but it can help your digestive issues. You just have to be careful when you ingest raw garlic. If you are unsure about eating it raw, slightly cooking it could help, however, its healing components will be reduced by the heat. If you are ready to ingest garlic raw, do so little by little. Eating garlic in large amounts at once will cause your stomach to become upset, you'll feel a burning sensation and the production of gas (interaction with the intestinal bacteria). You can enjoy the flavor and the health benefits of garlic by chopping raw cloves and then adding them at the last few minutes of cooking so you will still receive the healing benefits of the herb. You could also opt for garlic powder but take note of the manufacturer because the compounds you want may have been degraded during the drying and macerating process. You could also take garlic tablets or capsules. These preparations have enteric coatings, so stomach upset may be reduced.
